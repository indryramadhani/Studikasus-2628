Indonesia to Import More Fuel from the U.S.

Buletin News | May 15, 2025

BULETIN NEWS ‚Äî Indonesia plans to increase its imports of refined fuels, such as crude oil and liquefied petroleum gas (LPG), from the United States by approximately $10 billion. This strategic move aims to diversify Indonesia's energy sources and reduce reliance on traditional suppliers like Singapore, which currently accounts for about 54% of Indonesia's refined fuel needs.

The shift is also part of broader trade negotiations to address potential U.S. tariffs on Indonesian exports. By boosting imports from the U.S., Indonesia seeks to balance its trade surplus and avoid a potential 32% tariff on its goods. However, concerns have been raised regarding higher transportation and insurance costs associated with importing fuel from the U.S., which could impact the competitiveness of these imports.

State-owned energy company Pertamina is currently reviewing its import strategy and awaiting government direction to implement this shift effectively.
