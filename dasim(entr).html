Horror Film Dasim Premieres in Indonesian Theaters

Buletin News | May 20, 2025

BULETIN NEWS ‚Äî Indonesia‚Äôs film scene welcomes a new spine-chilling addition with the premiere of Dasim, a supernatural horror movie directed by Hadrah Daeng Ratu. Released in theaters nationwide this week, Dasim has already captured the attention of horror enthusiasts due to its eerie premise rooted in local folklore.

The story follows a young family that moves into a quiet suburban neighborhood, only to experience a series of unexplained events tied to the presence of a demonic entity named Dasim. According to Islamic tradition, Dasim is one of the five jinn offspring of Iblis, whose sole purpose is to ruin households and create chaos among families.

Starring Baim Wong, Mawar Eva de Jongh, and Anantya Kirana, the film blends psychological horror with traditional mysticism, offering a uniquely Indonesian horror experience. Director Hadrah Daeng Ratu emphasizes that Dasim is not only about scares but also serves as a reflection on family values and resilience in the face of adversity.

As of its opening weekend, the film has received strong word-of-mouth support, with audiences praising its suspenseful storytelling and eerie atmosphere. Dasim is expected to continue drawing large crowds and potentially spark renewed interest in horror films rooted in Indonesian mythology.
