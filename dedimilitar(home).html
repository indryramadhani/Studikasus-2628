List of Regions Participating in Dedi Mulyadi's Military School

Buletin News | May 6, 2025

BULETIN NEWS ‚Äî West Java Governor Dedi Mulyadi launched a program to develop troubled students through military-style education starting on May 2, 2025. This program aims to discipline students involved in juvenile delinquency, such as brawls, skipping school, and minor criminal acts.

The program involves several areas in West Java, including: Purwakarta: A total of 39 junior high school students have been sent to military barracks to take part in a coaching program. Bandung City: A total of 30 junior high and high school students are taking part in a similar program. Depok, Bogor, Cianjur, Garut, Tasikmalaya, Cimahi City, and Sukabumi: These areas have also sent students to military barracks as part of the coaching program.

The program is conducted at various military facilities across West Java, such as the Kostrad Regiment 1 in Purwakarta and the Rindam III/Siliwangi. Students will undergo training for a period ranging from 14 days to six months, depending on individual needs and evaluations.

Students receive a mix of physical, mental, and academic education, including: Nationalism and state defense, Military-style discipline and marching drills, Anti-drug education and religious instruction, Daily academic lessons aligned with the school curriculum (around 2 hours per day).

The program has sparked mixed reactions. Some see it as a bold solution to youth delinquency, while others express concern over potential human rights violations and the risk of militarizing civilian education.

Governor Dedi Mulyadi has responded by asserting that the program is conducted with full parental consent and aims to safeguard the broader community from the negative effects of youth misconduct.
