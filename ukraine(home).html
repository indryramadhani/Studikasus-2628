Ukraine war latest: Largest Drone Strike in Ukraine Since Beginning of War

Buletin News | May 15, 2025

BULETIN NEWS ‚Äî The armed conflict between Russiaand Ukraine that has been going on since February 2022 has again shown a major escalation. On a Sunday night in mid-May 2025, Russia launched the largest drone attack since the beginning of the invasion. A total of 273 kamikaze drones were launched into various regions of Ukraine, with the main focus on the capital, Kyiv.

The drones used in the attack were the Shahed, an Iranian-made kamikaze drone that Russia has used in previous attacks. But the number launched this time broke the record for airstrikes since the war began.

The attacks targeted civilian facilities, energy infrastructure, and residential areas in and around Kyiv. Initial reports indicate: (1) 88 drones were shot down by Ukrainian air defense systems, (2)Another 128 drones disappeared from radar and likely reached their targets or were not detected, (3)The attacks killed one person, and injured three others, including a 4-year-old child. Explosions occurred in several residential districts, causing damage to residential homes and local power grids.

Ukraine claims that its air defense systems have successfully intercepted most of the drones. However, the massive scale of the attacks has put a strain on existing defense systems. The Ukrainian government has stated that it needs more air defense assistance from partner countries such as the United States and the European Union.

Ukraine's Defense Minister said, This is not just an attack on Ukraine, but on the entire European security order. Russia is testing our resilience.

The international community, including NATO and the European Union, strongly condemned the attack. Countries including Germany, France and the UK called for additional sanctions on Russia and increased military and humanitarian support for Ukraine.

The UN also expressed concern over civilian casualties and called for an end to attacks on civilian infrastructure.
